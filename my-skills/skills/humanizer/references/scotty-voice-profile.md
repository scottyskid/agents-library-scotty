# Scotty's Writing Voice Profile

A personal writing profile for Scotty Skidmore. Use it with the humanizer skill to push
rewrites toward Scotty's real voice rather than a generic "human" voice. Australian
English throughout.

Snippets below are short (<15 words) and quoted only as evidence. Markers backed by one
sample are tagged [low confidence].

---

## Sentence length and rhythm

Mixed, but leans conversational-medium. Short reactive one-liners; longer winding
sentences when he explains reasoning, often chained with "and"/"if".

- Short and punchy: "Took that just then." / "Leave it with me for the moment."
- Long reasoning run-on, chained with *and*/*if*: "I wanted to validate if he was
  using it more often than others... if others were also facing this... or if there
  were some things I..."
- Softened long ask: "Its okay if you cant but we will likely go ahead with it anyway
  as I am keen to get the ball rolling."

He does not keep an even mid-length cadence; length tracks whether he's reacting or
reasoning.

## Paragraph length and structure

- **Chat:** one thought per message, fired in bursts of several short consecutive
  messages rather than one long block.
- **Email:** short paragraphs, usually one to two sentences, blank line between. Shape
  is greeting → a couple of short paragraphs → "Cheers".
- **Recaps:** bold section labels with short lines beneath, e.g. "**SSO migration**" /
  "**Grafana Cloud**" then a one-line status under each. Bold is used only for real
  section headers, not scattered through prose.

## Vocabulary

Plain, direct, business-casual. Technical terms appear naturally when relevant, but the
connective prose stays simple. No corporate inflation.

Words/phrases he reaches for:
- **"keen"** — his signature enthusiasm word. "I am keen to get your take...",
  "keen to get the ball rolling", "keen for you to be the primary driver". (many samples)
- **"touch base"** — "touch base tomorrow", "wanted to touch base on Platform Con".
- **"shoot me / shoot it over"** — "shoot me a calendar invite", "shoot it over and ill
  include it".
- "happy to" — "happy to wait", "happy to catch up when you are online".
- "Not a problem" / "Its fine" / "leave it with me".
- Warm reactions: "Awesome!", "Yep", "Huh", "hahaha", "Oh wow".
- "get the ball rolling", "light a fire under".

## Punctuation habits

- **Trailing off without a full stop.** Messages frequently end with no period: "...to
  accommodate", "same page with that".
- **Em/en dashes: essentially never.** He uses commas and full stops instead. (This
  matches the skill's hard no-dash rule.)
- **Exclamation marks:** used sparingly for warmth, not decoration — "Awesome!",
  "Thanks!". Never stacked.
- **Question marks:** asks direct questions freely; occasionally doubles for emphasis:
  "And why did you turn them down??"
- **Emoji:** occasional single friendly emoji, never on headings — "This is now working
  🙂". [low confidence — sparse in text]
- **Parenthetical asides:** "(us included)", "(maybe there are some default permissions
  that can be changed".

## Openers and sign-offs

- **Opener:** "Hey [Name]," is the default — "Hey Vincent,", "Hey Cullyn", "Hey Rach",
  "Hey Louis", "Hey Denis". Casual "Hey", not "Hi" or "Dear". Often "Hey, just wanted
  to..." / "Hey just something to bring to your attention".
- In chat he frequently skips the greeting and jumps straight into the point.
- **Sign-off:** "Cheers" is the habitual casual close. Email also carries a formal
  auto-signature ("Kind regards, / Scotty Skidmore / Director of Cloud Platform
  Engineering") — that block is boilerplate, not voice.
- **Transitions:** minimal connective scaffolding. He just starts the next point; where
  he does link, it's plain — "So Kevin is on leave...", "Plus I also found...".

## Tone by context

- **Email:** a touch more structured (greeting + Cheers) but still casual and plain.
- **Chat:** very casual, warm, direct; self-deprecating humour and jokes surface here.
- **Both:** a considerate manager voice — reassuring, protective of his team, softening
  asks. "I don't want people to be stressed about not being able to complete things."
  "Hibob is still quite new, do what you can with what you have at the moment."

## Confidence and hedging

Scotty is deliberately moving toward more confident, direct phrasing and away from
hedging. A small amount of softening is still natural and fine, but it should not pile
up.

- Keep it light and occasional: "which I believe is under Hosting", "hopefully more
  suitable time".
- Trim stacked qualifiers ("I think maybe we could possibly...") down to a single, or
  drop them and state the point directly.
- When he is being direct, let him be direct — pointed questions and plain statements
  are in character: "Why can so few tools get reporting right (us included)".

## Quirks

- **Contractions throughout:** "I'm", "don't", "cant", "its" — relaxed and
  conversational, not stiff.
- **Softened directives:** "feel free to", "I would encourage you to", "if you like the
  look of it" — rarely a bare command.
- **Self-deprecating warmth:** "sorry if I threw you more problems than solutions, I
  have a habit of doing that".
- **Dry humour / asides:** "Its fine, I didn't want to talk to either of you anyway",
  "the best way to phish me. The unsubscribe button...".
- **Pointed, sometimes blunt questions** to drive things: "Why can so few tools get
  reporting right (us included)", "And is this only for last month?".

## Things Scotty does NOT do (absent patterns)

- No em dashes or en dashes.
- No rule-of-three triads for a comprehensive feel.
- No corporate/AI inflation words (pivotal, testament, vibrant, landscape, leverage,
  seamless, robust).
- No signposting ("let's dive in", "here's what you need to know").
- No sycophantic openers ("Great question!", "You're absolutely right!").
- Rare semicolons; he prefers a full stop or a comma.
- No boldface scattered through prose (bold only for genuine recap section headers).
- No long throat-clearing intro — he opens with the point or a quick "Hey".

---

## Top markers to apply (highest confidence first)

When rewriting toward Scotty's voice, prioritise these:

1. Open casually with "Hey [Name]," and close with "Cheers" (email); in chat, skip the
   greeting and lead with the point.
2. Use "keen" for enthusiasm/intent, plus "touch base", "shoot it over", "happy to",
   "leave it with me".
3. Keep sentences plain and direct; let reasoning sentences run long with "and"/"if"
   rather than splitting into tidy equal-length lines.
4. Soften asks and directives — "feel free to", "do what you can", "I would encourage
   you to" — but keep the underlying point confident.
5. Lean confident: state things directly and trim hedging. A single light softener is
   fine; don't stack qualifiers.
6. Reassuring, people-first manager tone; a little dry self-deprecating humour is in
   character.
7. Contractions throughout; keep it relaxed and conversational, not stiff.
8. Never add em/en dashes, rule-of-three, corporate inflation, or signposting.
